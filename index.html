Hi, I'm Gil

When I was 22 I spent a summer backpacking in Alaska.

I thought about quitting and heading back home every day. But despite the endless swarms of greedy mosquitoes, constant feeling of exhaustion, dozens of injuries, and missing my family and friends, I kept going. I said to myself that I was going to do this difficult thing and I followed through. Now, looking back, it's one of the most rewarding experiences I've ever had.

Driven by finding patterns, optimizing solutions to increase efficiency, and constantly pushing myself to improve, I put my curiosity and perseverance to work as a full stack developer. There might not be as much physical difficulty in the tech scene, but there are plenty of other exciting challenges to tackle! Constantly picking up new information and facing one problem after another is what I thrive on.

As a former startup co-founder in the game industry, I was fortunate to get plenty of hands-on technical experience as well as bigger picture skills including: planning complex projects ahead of time, managing and working along different departments, and communicating effectively in technical terms.

If you're interested in meeting up and talking about tech, design, or the plethora of mosquitoes in Alaska, please don't hesitate to reach out.

InkyTweet

InkyTweet lets users login through their Twitter accounts. Once logged in, they can browse through "ghost" writers and see a sample of their work. If they like one, they can subscribe to have access to that writer's tweets on offer. They can then store and tweet directly from InkyTweet.

Alternatively, writers can create tweets and showcase them on their profile pages.

Based on their influence on Twitter, users are given a reputation score which determines their channel's subscription cost.

InkyTweet was developed with Lucas Santini.
